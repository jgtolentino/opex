# Travel and Entertainment Policy

## Policy Statement

This policy establishes guidelines for business travel and entertainment expenses to ensure cost-effectiveness, employee safety, compliance with tax regulations, and appropriate business conduct. All employees must adhere to these standards when traveling or entertaining for business purposes.

**Policy Number**: FIN-006
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
**Last Reviewed**: January 15, 2025
**Policy Owner**: Chief Financial Officer
**Approval Authority**: CEO

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees traveling on company business
- All business-related entertainment and client meals
- Domestic and international travel
- Both pre-approved and reimbursement-based expenses

---

## General Principles

### 1. Business Necessity
All travel and entertainment must have clear business purpose and be necessary for conducting company business.

### 2. Cost-Effectiveness
Employees expected to exercise reasonable judgment and choose economical options without compromising safety or business effectiveness.

### 3. Pre-Approval
Certain travel and entertainment require advance approval (see matrices below).

### 4. Safety First
Employee safety takes priority over cost considerations.

### 5. Compliance
All expenses must comply with tax regulations, anti-corruption laws, and company policies.

## Travel Authorization

### Pre-Approval Requirements

| Travel Type | Pre-Approval Required | Submitted To | Timeline |
|------------|----------------------|--------------|----------|
| **Domestic (1-2 days)** | Manager notification | Manager | 3 days advance |
| **Domestic (3+ days)** | Manager approval | Manager | 5 days advance |
| **Domestic (>₱50K cost)** | Dept Head approval | Manager + Dept Head | 7 days advance |
| **International (Asia)** | Dept Head + HR | Manager + HR | 14 days advance |
| **International (Other)** | Dept Head + CFO + HR | Multi-level | 21 days advance |
| **Conference (>₱50K)** | Dept Head + Finance | Finance + Dept Head | 14 days advance |

**Emergency Travel**: Manager approval can be verbal, with written follow-up within 24 hours.

## Air Travel

### Domestic Flights (Philippines)

| Travel Distance/Duration | Class Allowed | Booking Window | Notes |
|-------------------------|--------------|----------------|-------|
| **<1 hour flight** | Economy | 14 days advance | Preferred airlines: PAL, Cebu Pacific |
| **>1 hour flight** | Economy | 14 days advance | Premium economy at personal expense |
| **Senior leadership (VP+)** | Flexible | 7 days advance | Premium economy allowed |

**Booking Requirements**:
- Use corporate travel partner (if available)
- Compare at least 2 airlines
- Choose most economical option considering timing
- Direct flights preferred (unless significant savings)

---

### International Flights

| Flight Duration | Standard Class | Exceptions | Approval Required |
|----------------|---------------|------------|------------------|
| **<4 hours** | Economy | - | None |
| **4-8 hours** | Economy | Premium economy (VP+ or medical) | Dept Head |
| **>8 hours** | Premium Economy | Business class (C-level or medical) | CFO |
| **>12 hours** | Premium Economy | Business class allowed | CFO |

**Upgrade Policy**:
- Personal upgrades: Employee expense
- Loyalty program upgrades: Allowed and encouraged
- Paid upgrades: Require approval per matrix

**Baggage**: Standard checked bag included; excess baggage at personal expense unless business-justified.

---

### Booking Procedures

**Corporate Travel Partner** (preferred):
- Access through company portal
- Preferred pricing and terms
- Automatic policy compliance
- Integrated expense reporting

**Self-Booking** (if approved):
- Manager approval required
- Must meet policy guidelines
- Receipt required for reimbursement
- No premium upgrades

**Change and Cancellation**:
- Notify travel partner immediately
- Change fees reimbursable if business-related
- Personal changes: Employee expense
- Travel insurance: Included on international trips

## Accommodation

### Hotel Standards

| Location | Per Night Limit | Hotel Standard | Notes |
|---------|----------------|----------------|-------|
| **Metro Manila** | ₱5,000 | 3-4 star business hotel | BGC, Makati, Ortigas |
| **Provincial Cities** | ₱3,500 | 3 star hotel | Clean, safe, central |
| **International - Asia** | $150 USD | 3-4 star | City center/business district |
| **International - US/Europe** | $200 USD | 3-4 star | Adjusted for high-cost cities |
| **International - Other** | $120 USD | 3 star | Safe location priority |

**High-Cost Cities** (Adjustment +30%):
- Singapore, Hong Kong, Tokyo
- London, Paris, Zurich
- New York, San Francisco

**Booking Requirements**:
- Standard/business room only
- Breakfast included preferred
- Close to business location
- Safety and reputation verified
- Corporate rates when available

**Ineligible**:
- Luxury/5-star hotels (without CFO approval)
- Resort properties (unless event location)
- Room upgrades
- Spa services, minibar, movies
- Late checkout fees

---

### Alternative Accommodation

**Serviced Apartments** (for trips >7 days):
- More cost-effective
- Kitchen facilities available
- Manager approval required
- Must be safe and reputable

**Homestays/Airbnb**:
- Allowed for >5 night stays if cost-effective
- Safety and legitimacy verified
- Business address for receipts
- Limit: ₱4,000/night domestic, $130/night international

---

## Ground Transportation

### Local Transportation

| Method | Reimbursement | Limits | Documentation |
|--------|---------------|--------|---------------|
| **Taxi/Ride-Hailing** | Full | ₱500 per trip | Receipt required >₱200 |
| **Personal Vehicle** | Mileage | ₱15 per km | Mileage log required |
| **Parking** | Full | Actual cost | Receipt required |
| **Public Transport** | Full | Actual cost | Minimal documentation |
| **Car Rental** | Pre-approved | Economy class | Manager approval |

**Ride-Hailing Apps** (Grab, Uber):
- Standard/economy service only
- Premium services at personal expense
- Proper receipts required

**Personal Vehicle**:
- Mileage calculated via Google Maps
- Parking and tolls reimbursable
- Fuel not separately reimbursable (included in mileage rate)
- Insurance: Personal policy must be active

---

### Car Rental

**When Allowed**:
- Multiple meetings in dispersed locations
- Public transport inadequate
- Cost-effective vs. taxis (>4 trips/day)
- Group travel (2+ employees)

**Requirements**:
- Pre-approval from manager
- Economy/compact class
- Corporate rental partner preferred
- Full insurance coverage required

**Ineligible**:
- Luxury/premium vehicles
- GPS rental (use mobile phone)
- Fuel upgrades/prepayment (unless required)

## Meals and Per Diem

### Meal Allowances (Within Philippines)

| Meal | Allowance | Conditions |
|------|-----------|------------|
| **Breakfast** | ₱300 | If hotel breakfast not included |
| **Lunch** | ₱500 | Business day |
| **Dinner** | ₱700 | Overnight travel |
| **Daily Total** | ₱1,500 | Composite per diem option |

**Per Diem Option**:
- Receive ₱1,500/day (domestic) fixed allowance
- No receipts required
- Simpler for traveler and finance
- Covers all meals and incidentals

**Actual Expense Option**:
- Submit receipts for reimbursement
- Individual meal limits apply
- Flexibility for higher/lower costs
- More administrative work

**Election**: Choose per diem or actual at trip start; cannot mix.

---

| Region | Per Diem (USD/day) | Covers |
|--------|-------------------|--------|

| **Other** | $65 | Other destinations |

**Inclusions**: Meals, tips, incidentals (laundry, local calls)

**Exclusions**: Alcohol (separate approval), entertainment, personal items

## Client Entertainment

### Entertainment Guidelines

| Event Type | Per Person Limit | Pre-Approval | Documentation |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Business Meals** | ₱2,000 | Manager (>₱10K total) | Attendee list |
| **Client Dinners** | ₱3,500 | Manager (>₱20K total) | Detailed |
| **Drinks/Cocktails** | ₱1,000 | Manager | Business purpose |
| **Events/Shows** | ₱5,000 | Dept Head | Client relationship |
| **Golf/Sports** | ₱8,000 | Dept Head | Strategic clients |

**Required Documentation**:
1. Names and companies of all attendees
2. Business purpose/relationship
3. Discussion topics (high-value events)
4. Expected business outcome

---

### Alcohol Policy

**General Rule**: Moderate alcohol consumption acceptable in business entertainment context.

**Limits**:
- ₱300 per person as part of meal
- ₱1,000 per person for cocktail event
- Must not impair business judgment
- Never during work hours (except client entertainment)

**Approval**:
- Included in meal budget: No separate approval
- Standalone drinks: Manager approval
- Significant alcohol component (>30% of bill): Dept Head

**Prohibited**:
- Excessive drinking
- Alcohol-only events without food
- Entertainment of government officials involving alcohol (anti-corruption risk)

---

### Gift Giving

**Client/Partner Gifts**:

| Gift Value | Occasion | Approval Required |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| **Up to ₱2,000** | Holidays, appreciation | Manager notification |
| **₱2,001 - ₱5,000** | Significant milestones | Manager approval |
| **Over ₱5,000** | Major contracts, partnerships | Dept Head + CFO |

**Requirements**:
- Recipient name and company documented
- Business justification
- Culturally appropriate
- No cash or cash-equivalents
- Compliant with recipient's gift policy

**Prohibited Recipients** (without legal counsel):
- Government officials
- Regulators
- Anyone who could influence business decisions improperly

## Conferences and Training

### Event Attendance

**Approval Requirements**:

| Cost Category | Includes | Approval |
|--------------|---------|----------|
| **<₱20,000** | Registration, local travel | Manager |
| **₱20,001 - ₱50,000** | Registration + domestic travel | Dept Head |
| **Over ₱50,000** | International events | Dept Head + Finance |

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Relevance to job role
- Professional development value
- Business benefit to company
- Budget availability
- Alternative learning options

---

### Conference Expenses

**Covered**:
- Registration/conference fees
- Travel and accommodation per policy
- Business meals (outside provided meals)
- Required conference materials

**Not Covered**:
- Spouse/companion travel
- Personal sightseeing
- Premium event packages
- Social events beyond core conference

**Post-Event Requirement**:
- Share key learnings with team (presentation or memo)
- Apply knowledge to work
- Consider presenting at future events (if relevant)

## International Travel Specific

### Travel Documentation

**Required**:
- Valid passport (6 months validity)
- Visa (if required - processed by company)
- Travel insurance (company-provided)
- Emergency contact card

**Company Responsibilities**:
- Visa application support
- Travel insurance (medical, evacuation)
- Emergency assistance hotline
- Country-specific safety briefings

---

### Health and Safety

**Pre-Travel**:
- Review travel advisories (DFA, US State Dept)
- Required vaccinations (company-reimbursed)
- Health insurance confirmation
- Emergency contact registration

**During Travel**:
- Daily check-in with manager (if high-risk location)
- Awareness of local laws and customs
- Secure handling of company information
- Emergency assistance hotline: +63-XXX-XXXX (24/7)

---

### Currency and Exchange

**Foreign Currency**:
- Expenses in local currency converted at Philippine Central Bank (BSP) rate
- Credit card exchange rates acceptable
- Attach currency conversion documentation
- Large cash exchanges: Obtain bank receipt

**Corporate Credit Card**:
- International travel: Use corporate card when possible
- No foreign transaction fees on corporate cards
- Automatic expense integration
- Better fraud protection

## Expense Reporting

### Submission Timeframe

| Travel Type | Submission Deadline |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Domestic (1-2 days)** | Within 10 business days of return |
| **Domestic (3+ days)** | Within 15 business days of return |

## Traveler Safety and Wellbeing

### Travel Risk Assessment

**Risk Levels**:
- **Low**: Standard business travel, developed countries
- **Medium**: Developing countries, infrastructure challenges
- **High**: Travel advisories, political instability, health risks

**High-Risk Travel**:
- CFO approval required
- Comprehensive briefing
- Enhanced security measures
- Daily check-ins mandatory
- Evacuation plan in place

---

### Travel Assistance

**24/7 Emergency Hotline**: +63-XXX-XXXX

**Services**:
- Medical emergency assistance
- Legal assistance
- Lost passport/documents
- Emergency evacuation
- Security incidents

**Traveler Responsibilities**:
- Keep emergency card accessible
- Notify manager of safe arrival
- Follow safety protocols
- Report incidents immediately

## Policy Exceptions

### Exception Request Process

**Situations**:
- Medical needs requiring upgraded travel
- Exceptional client relationship requirements
- Remote locations with limited options
- Force majeure/emergency

**Process**:
1. Written request to manager
2. Detailed justification
3. Alternative options considered
4. CFO approval for material exceptions (>₱50K)

---

### Frequent Traveler Considerations

**Employees Traveling >10 trips/year**:
- Eligible for lounge access (if cost-effective)
- Corporate credit card priority
- Flexible booking arrangements
- Accumulated miles personal benefit

**Note**: Company pays base fare; traveler keeps loyalty benefits.

|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------|

| **Unapproved upgrade** | Personal payment | Suspension of travel privileges | Disciplinary action |
| **Excessive entertainment** | Partial reimbursement | Written warning | Termination consideration |
| **Fraudulent expense** | Immediate termination | Legal action | Criminal prosecution |
