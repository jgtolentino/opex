# Budget Approval Authority Matrix Policy

## Policy Statement

This policy establishes clear financial authority limits and approval requirements for budget allocation, expenditure approval, and financial commitments. This ensures appropriate oversight, financial control, and accountability at all organizational levels.

**Policy Number**: FIN-005
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
**Last Reviewed**: January 15, 2025
**Policy Owner**: Chief Financial Officer
**Approval Authority**: CEO & Board of Directors

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All budget-related approvals
- All financial commitments and expenditures
- All employees with spending authority
- All business units and departments
- All entities within the organization

---

## Approval Authority Matrix

### Operational Expenditures

|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|-------|
| **Team Member** | ₱5,000 | ₱20,000 | - | Manager approval required |
| **Team Lead** | ₱25,000 | ₱100,000 | ₱500,000 | Department Head oversight |
| **Manager** | ₱100,000 | ₱500,000 | ₱2,000,000 | Within approved budget |
| **Senior Manager** | ₱250,000 | ₱1,000,000 | ₱5,000,000 | Finance review >₱100K |
| **Department Head** | ₱500,000 | ₱2,000,000 | ₱10,000,000 | CFO approval >₱250K |

### Capital Expenditures (CapEx)

| Purchase Amount | Approval Required | Additional Requirements |
|----------------|------------------|------------------------|
| Up to ₱100,000 | Department Head | Business justification |
| ₱100,001 - ₱500,000 | Director + Finance Controller | ROI analysis |
| ₱500,001 - ₱2,000,000 | VP + CFO | Detailed business case |
| ₱2,000,001 - ₱10,000,000 | CEO + CFO | Board notification |
| Over ₱10,000,000 | Board of Directors | Formal board resolution |

**CapEx Definition**: Expenditures for assets with useful life >1 year and value >₱50,000

### Budget Transfers and Reallocations

| Reallocation Amount | Approval Required | Restrictions |
|--------------------|------------------|-------------|
| Up to ₱50,000 | Budget Owner (same department) | Within OpEx categories |
| ₱50,001 - ₱250,000 | Department Head | Within same division |
| ₱250,001 - ₱1,000,000 | Director + Finance Controller | Cross-departmental allowed |
| Over ₱1,000,000 | CFO + CEO | Material budget change |

**Restrictions**:
- Cannot transfer from OpEx to CapEx (requires new approval)
- Cannot exceed total approved budget
- No retroactive reallocations (pre-approval required)

## Budget Planning and Approval Process

### Annual Budget Cycle

| Timeline | Activity | Responsible | Deliverable |
|----------|---------|------------|-------------|
| **August** | Budget guidelines issued | Finance Team | Budget template, assumptions |
| **September** | Department budget drafts | Department Heads | Preliminary budgets |
| **October** | Budget review meetings | Finance + Dept Heads | Revised budgets |
| **November** | Consolidated budget | Finance Team | Company-wide budget |
| **November (late)** | Executive review | CFO + CEO | Final budget recommendation |
| **December** | Board approval | Board of Directors | Approved annual budget |
| **January 1** | Budget effective | All | Budget loaded in Clarity PPM |

---

### Budget Approval Criteria

**Department Budgets Evaluated On**:
1. Strategic alignment with company goals
2. Revenue assumptions reasonableness
3. Cost efficiency and productivity
4. ROI on major initiatives
5. Headcount justification
6. CapEx prioritization
7. Risk assessment and contingencies

**Approval Gates**:
- Department Head: Owns department budget
- Finance Controller: Reviews all department budgets
- CFO: Consolidates and recommends
- CEO: Approves company budget
- Board: Final approval authority

---

### Mid-Year Budget Review

**Timing**: July (Q2 close)

**Actions**:
1. Actual vs. budget variance analysis
2. Forecast update (rolling 12 months)
3. Identify material variances (>15% or >₱500K)
4. Propose budget adjustments if needed
5. Reaffirm or revise full-year guidance

**Approval**: CFO (adjustments <₱2M), CEO + Board (>₱2M)

---

## Budget Monitoring and Controls

### Variance Reporting

**Monthly Reporting**:
- Actual vs. budget by department
- Variance explanations for >10% or >₱100K
- Rolling forecast (3 months ahead)
- Key metrics and KPIs

**Recipients**: Department Heads, Executive Team, Board (summary)

---

### Budget Over-runs

**Protocol**:

| Variance Level | Action Required |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **<5% variance** | No action (normal fluctuation) |
| **5-10% variance** | Manager explanation required |
| **10-20% variance** | Dept Head review, Finance notification |
| **>20% variance** | CFO review, mitigation plan required |

**Consequences of Unapproved Over-runs**:
- Spending freeze until resolved
- Reallocation from other budget lines
- Deferral of non-critical expenses
- Performance review impact (if habitual)

---

### Spending Freeze Conditions

**Automatic Freeze Triggered By**:
- Quarterly revenue miss >15%
- Cash reserves below 3 months operating expenses
- Unexpected major expense (>₱5M)
- CEO/CFO directive

**Freeze Levels**:
1. **Soft Freeze**: Non-critical expenses deferred, CFO approval required for exceptions
2. **Hard Freeze**: All non-committed expenses stopped, CEO approval only
3. **Emergency Freeze**: Only critical operations spending, Board oversight

## Delegation of Authority

### Temporary Delegation

**Process**:
1. Written delegation (email or system notification)
2. Specify scope, duration, and limits
3. Finance team notified
4. Approval workflow updated in systems

**Maximum Duration**: 30 days (renewable)

**Valid Reasons**:
- Vacation/leave
- Extended business travel
- Illness
- Sabbatical

---

### Acting Role Authority

**Principles**:
- Acting manager assumes approval authority of role
- Requires formal acting role assignment
- HR and Finance notified
- System access updated

**Timeframe**: Effective during entire acting period

---

## Special Approval Situations

### Urgent Expenditure (Cannot Wait for Approval)

**Protocol**:
1. Verbal approval from next level authority (documented)
2. Written justification within 24 hours
3. Formal approval processed within 48 hours
4. Post-approval review by Finance

**Valid Urgency Reasons**:
- Safety/security risk
- System outage affecting operations
- Legal/regulatory deadline
- Customer commitment
- Force majeure

---

### Retrospective Approval (Discouraged)

**Limited Circumstances**:
- Emergency spending (pre-approved verbally)
- System error or process failure
- Exceptional customer situation

**Process**:
1. Explanation of circumstances
2. Justification why advance approval impossible
3. CFO review required (all cases)
4. Documentation of corrective actions

**Note**: Habitual retrospective approvals subject to disciplinary action

## Compliance and Audit

### Internal Controls

**Segregation of Duties**:
- Requester ≠ Approver
- Approver ≠ Payment Processor
- Budget Owner ≠ Budget Approver

**System Controls**:
- Approval workflows enforced in Clarity PPM and ServiceNow
- Budget availability checked automatically
- Approval limits configured per this policy
- Audit trail maintained for all approvals

---

### Audit Requirements

**Internal Audit** (Quarterly):
- Sample testing of approval compliance
- Budget variance analysis
- Exception review
- Delegation verification

**External Audit** (Annual):
- Management override testing
- Authority matrix compliance
- Contract signing authority verification
- Financial statement impact assessment

---

### Non-Compliance Consequences

| Violation Type | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Exceeded authority (minor)** | Written warning | Suspension of authority | Termination consideration |
| **Exceeded authority (major)** | Formal reprimand | Suspension | Termination |
| **Fraudulent approval** | Immediate termination | Legal action | Criminal prosecution |
| **Circumventing controls** | Written warning | Suspension of authority | Termination |

**Restitution**: Personal liability for unauthorized expenses

---

## Policy Exceptions

**Exception Request**:
- Submitted in writing to CFO
- Detailed justification
- Risk assessment
- One-level-up approval required

**Reporting**: All exceptions logged and reported quarterly to Board
