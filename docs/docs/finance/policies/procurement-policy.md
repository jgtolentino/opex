# Procurement and Vendor Management Policy

## Policy Statement

This policy establishes guidelines for the procurement of goods and services to ensure value for money, transparency, compliance with regulations, and effective vendor management. All purchases must follow these procedures to maintain financial controls and audit compliance.

**Policy Number**: PROC-001
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
**Last Reviewed**: January 15, 2025
**Policy Owner**: Chief Financial Officer
**Approval Authority**: CEO & Board of Directors

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All purchases of goods and services
- All employees authorized to make purchases
- All vendors and suppliers
- All business entities within the organization

**Exceptions**: Emergency purchases (see Section 9)

---

## Procurement Principles

### 1. Value for Money
Maximize value through competitive pricing, quality, and total cost of ownership.

### 2. Transparency
All procurement processes must be fair, open, and documented.

### 3. Competition
Solicit competitive quotes to ensure best pricing and terms.

### 4. Ethical Conduct
No conflicts of interest; comply with anti-corruption laws.

### 5. Sustainability
Consider environmental and social impacts where feasible.

## Procurement Authority Matrix

| Purchase Amount | Quote Requirements | Approval Required | Purchase Order |
|----------------|-------------------|------------------|----------------|
| Up to ₱10,000 | 1 quote (informal) | Manager | Optional |
| ₱10,001 - ₱50,000 | 2 written quotes | Department Head | Required |
| ₱50,001 - ₱250,000 | 3 written quotes | Finance Controller | Required |
| ₱250,001 - ₱1,000,000 | 3 formal proposals | CFO | Required |
| Over ₱1,000,000 | Formal tender/RFP | CEO + Board | Required |

## Procurement Process

### Step 1: Needs Assessment
**Responsible**: Requester

**Actions**:
1. Define clear business need
2. Develop specifications or scope
3. Determine budget availability
4. Consider alternatives (lease vs. buy, used vs. new)
5. Assess urgency and timeline

**Output**: Business justification document

---

### Step 2: Vendor Selection
**Responsible**: Procurement Team (or Requester for <₱50K)

**Approved Vendor List**:
- Preference for approved vendors
- Faster processing
- Pre-vetted for insurance, tax compliance
- Established payment terms

**New Vendor Evaluation Criteria**:
- Financial stability
- Technical capability
- References and track record
- Insurance and certifications
- Tax compliance (BIR registration)
- Payment terms
- Geographic location and delivery capability

---

### Step 3: Quote Solicitation

| Purchase Amount | Process |
|----------------|---------|
| **Up to ₱10,000** | Informal quote (email, phone) acceptable |
| **₱10,001 - ₱50,000** | Written quotes from 2 vendors |
| **₱50,001 - ₱250,000** | Formal written quotes from 3 vendors |
| **Over ₱250,000** | Request for Proposal (RFP) or tender |

**Quote Validity**: Minimum 30 days

**Quote Comparison Criteria**:
- Total cost (including delivery, taxes, fees)
- Quality and specifications compliance
- Delivery timeline
- Payment terms
- Warranty and support
- Vendor reputation and capability

---

### Step 4: Purchase Request Submission
**Responsible**: Requester

**Required Information**:
1. Item/service description and specifications
2. Quantity and unit price
3. Total cost (including taxes)
4. Budget code from Clarity PPM
5. Business justification
6. Vendor quotes (as per matrix)
7. Preferred vendor and rationale
8. Delivery deadline

**System**: Submit via ServiceNow purchase request module

---

### Step 5: Approval Process
**Responsible**: Approvers per authority matrix

**Approval Criteria**:
- Business necessity
- Budget availability
- Competitive pricing
- Vendor suitability
- Compliance with procurement policy

**Timeframes**:
- Manager approval: 2 business days
- Department Head: 2 business days
- Finance Controller: 2 business days
- CFO: 3 business days

See: [Purchase Request Workflow](/docs/finance/workflows/purchase-request)

---

### Step 6: Purchase Order Issuance
**Responsible**: Procurement Team

**PO Contents**:
- PO number (unique)
- Vendor details
- Item descriptions and quantities
- Unit prices and total amount
- Delivery terms (Incoterms)
- Payment terms
- Delivery address and contact
- Special instructions

**PO Distribution**:
- Vendor (via email)
- Requester (for goods receipt)
- Accounts Payable (for invoice matching)
- Archive (for audit trail)

---

### Step 7: Goods Receipt
**Responsible**: Requester

**Actions**:
1. Verify delivery against PO
2. Inspect for quality and damage
3. Confirm quantity received
4. Record receipt in system (within 24 hours)
5. Notify AP if issues found

**Delivery Discrepancies**:
- Partial delivery: Note on receipt
- Damaged goods: Reject and document
- Wrong items: Contact vendor immediately

---

### Step 8: Invoice Processing & Payment
**Responsible**: Accounts Payable

**3-Way Match**:
1. Purchase Order
2. Goods Receipt
3. Vendor Invoice

**Payment Terms** (standard):
- Net 30 days from invoice date
- Early payment discount if >2%
- Payment via bank transfer (preferred)

See: [Invoice Processing Workflow](/docs/finance/workflows/invoice-processing)

## Vendor Management

### Vendor Onboarding

**New Vendor Requirements**:
1. **Tax Compliance**
   - BIR Certificate of Registration
   - Valid TIN
   - BIR Form 2303 (if applicable)

2. **Business Documentation**
   - Business registration
   - Mayor's permit
   - SEC/DTI registration

3. **Insurance** (for certain categories)
   - Liability insurance (construction, events)
   - Product liability (goods suppliers)

4. **Banking Information**
   - Bank account details
   - Account verification letter

**Onboarding Timeline**: 5-7 business days

---

### Vendor Performance Evaluation

**Evaluation Frequency**: Quarterly (major vendors) or Annually (others)

**Criteria**:
| Metric | Weight | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Quality | 30% | Defect rate, returns |
| Delivery | 25% | On-time delivery rate |
| Pricing | 20% | Competitiveness, value |
| Responsiveness | 15% | Issue resolution time |
| Compliance | 10% | Documentation, terms adherence |

**Rating Scale**:
- Excellent (90-100%): Preferred vendor status
- Good (75-89%): Approved vendor
- Fair (60-74%): Review and improvement plan
- Poor (<60%): Probation or removal

---

### Vendor Contract Management

**Contract Required For**:
- Recurring services
- Annual purchases >₱500,000
- Critical suppliers
- Service agreements

**Contract Terms to Include**:
- Scope of work/specifications
- Pricing and payment terms
- Delivery/performance schedules
- Quality standards
- Warranties and guarantees
- Liability and indemnification
- Termination clauses
- Dispute resolution

**Contract Approval**:
- Legal review required for >₱1M or >1 year
- CFO approval required
- CEO approval for >₱5M

---

## Special Procurement Categories

### IT Hardware and Software

**Additional Requirements**:
- IT department approval mandatory
- Security assessment for software
- Licensing compliance review
- Integration compatibility check
- Data privacy assessment

**Preferred Vendors**: Use corporate IT partners when available

---

### Professional Services

**Examples**: Legal, consulting, audit, marketing

**Selection Process**:
1. Scope of work development
2. Proposal solicitation (min 3 firms for >₱250K)
3. Evaluation criteria:
   - Technical expertise (40%)
   - Experience and references (30%)
   - Pricing (20%)
   - Cultural fit (10%)
4. Contract negotiation

**Engagement Letter**: Required for all professional services

---

### Construction and Facilities

**Additional Requirements**:
- Licensed contractor
- Performance bond (for >₱1M)
- Safety certifications
- Insurance certificates
- Project timeline and milestones

**Progress Payments**: Based on milestone completion (max 20% advance)

---

## Conflict of Interest

### Declaration Required

Employees must declare if:
- They have personal/family relationship with vendor
- They have financial interest in vendor company
- They will receive personal benefit from transaction

**Process**:
1. Written disclosure to manager and procurement
2. Recusal from selection process
3. Additional approval required
4. May require external review

**Penalties**: Disciplinary action up to termination, legal action if fraudulent

---

## Prohibited Practices

❌ Splitting purchases to avoid approval thresholds
❌ Personal purchases through company accounts
❌ Accepting gifts/kickbacks from vendors (>₱2,000 value)
❌ Favoritism without business justification
❌ Undisclosed conflicts of interest
❌ Verbal commitments without proper approval
❌ Altering quotes or specifications
❌ Payments to government officials without legal basis

**Consequence**: Termination and legal action

---

## Emergency Procurement

### Definition
Urgent purchases required due to:
- System/equipment failure affecting operations
- Safety or security risk
- Legal/regulatory deadline
- Force majeure event

### Process
1. **Verbal approval** from CFO or delegate
2. **Document emergency justification**
3. **Submit formal purchase request within 24 hours**
4. **Post-approval review** within 5 business days

**Note**: Emergency designation cannot be used routinely or for poor planning

---

## Sustainable Procurement

### Considerations (when feasible)

**Environmental**:
- Energy-efficient products
- Recycled/recyclable materials
- Minimal packaging
- Local sourcing (reduced transport)

**Social**:
- Fair labor practices
- Diverse suppliers (SMEs, women-owned)
- Community impact

**Note**: Sustainability considered but not at excessive cost premium (>10%)

## Procurement Reporting

### Monthly Reports
- Purchase volume by category
- Vendor spend analysis
- Savings achieved
- Purchase order aging

### Quarterly Reports
- Vendor performance ratings
- Contract renewals pending
- Policy compliance rate
- Procurement cycle time

**Distribution**: CFO, Executive Team, Board (quarterly summary)

---

## Audit and Compliance

**Internal Audit**:
- Random sampling of purchases (10% quarterly)
- Compliance with approval matrix
- Competitive quote adherence
- Vendor documentation completeness

**External Audit**:
- Annual financial audit includes procurement testing
- All documentation must be available
- 7-year retention requirement

---

## Policy Exceptions

**Exception Request**:
- Written justification to Finance Controller
- CFO approval required
- Documented and reported quarterly

**Valid Exception Reasons**:
- Sole source vendor (with justification)
- Proprietary product/service
- Emergency circumstances
- Strategic partnership
- Government-mandated supplier
