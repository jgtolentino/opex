**Document Control**
- **Document ID:** FIN-POL-001
- **Version:** 1.0
- **Effective Date:** 2025-11-15
- **Review Cycle:** Annual
- **Policy Owner:** Chief Financial Officer
- **Process Owner:** Finance Director (GVKC)

## 1. Executive Summary

This document establishes the governance framework, regulatory requirements, and internal control policies for the month-end financial closing process. It ensures compliance with Philippine Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) regulations, Philippine Financial Reporting Standards (PFRS), and internal control best practices aligned with SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley) principles.

**Purpose:**
- Define regulatory compliance requirements
- Establish internal control framework
- Document audit trail and evidence requirements
- Set retention policies
- Define escalation and exception handling protocols

**Scope:**
- All month-end closing activities
- All BIR tax compliance requirements
- All financial reporting and controls
- All entities under the organization

#### 2.1.2 Tax Rates (As of 2025)

**Value-Added Tax (VAT):**
- Standard Rate: **12%** on sale of goods, services, and importation
- Zero-Rated: Exports and specified transactions
- VAT-Exempt: Specified goods and services under the Tax Code

**Corporate Income Tax:**
- **25%** for corporations with net taxable income not exceeding PHP 5 million AND total assets not exceeding PHP 100 million
- **30%** for all other corporations
- Minimum Corporate Income Tax (MCIT): **2%** of gross income (after Year 4 of operations)

**Withholding Tax Rates (Expanded):**
- Professional fees (professionals): **5% / 10%** (depending on annual gross income)
- Professional fees (non-professionals): **10% / 15%**
- Rent on real property: **5%**
- Commissions: **10%**
- Service fees: Varies (1% to 15%) per RR 2-98 as amended

#### 2.1.3 Invoicing and Documentation Requirements

**VAT Invoice Requirements (per RR 18-2012):**
1. The word "VAT Invoice" or "VAT Official Receipt"
2. TIN of seller
3. Name, business name, address of seller
4. Date of transaction
5. Quantity, unit cost, description of goods/services
6. TIN of purchaser (if required)
7. Gross selling price or gross value of services
8. VAT amount (separately stated) or "VAT-inclusive" or "VAT Zero-Rated"
9. BIR permit to print number and details

**Invoice Retention:**
- **10 years** from date of transaction (per Tax Code Section 235)

#### 2.1.4 Electronic Filing Requirements

**BIR eFPS (Electronic Filing and Payment System):**
- Mandatory for all corporations and taxpayers registered as large taxpayers
- All returns must be filed electronically
- Payments must be made through authorized agent banks or online banking

### 2.2 Philippine Financial Reporting Standards (PFRS/PAS)

#### 2.2.1 Revenue Recognition (PFRS 15)

**For Service Contracts:**
- Recognize revenue when (or as) performance obligations are satisfied
- Use **Percentage of Completion (POC)** method for long-term contracts
- Measure progress using input method (costs incurred) or output method (milestones achieved)

**Five-Step Model:**
1. Identify the contract(s) with a customer
2. Identify the performance obligations in the contract
3. Determine the transaction price
4. Allocate the transaction price to the performance obligations
5. Recognize revenue when (or as) the entity satisfies a performance obligation

**WIP/POP Accounting:**
- **Unbilled Receivables:** Revenue recognized but not yet billed
- **Deferred Revenue:** Advance billings not yet earned
- Reconcile monthly to ensure accurate revenue recognition

#### 2.2.2 Lease Accounting (PFRS 16)

**For All Leases (except short-term <12 months and low-value):**
- Recognize **Right-of-Use (ROU) Asset** and **Lease Liability** on commencement
- Measure lease liability at present value of lease payments
- Depreciate ROU asset over lease term or useful life (whichever is shorter)
- Recognize interest expense on lease liability

#### 2.2.3 Financial Instruments (PFRS 9)

**Impairment of Financial Assets:**
- Apply **Expected Credit Loss (ECL)** model for trade receivables
- Simplified approach: Lifetime ECL for trade receivables without significant financing component
- Provision matrix based on aging and historical loss rates

**Recommended Provision Rates:**
- 0-30 days: 0%
- 31-60 days: 5%
- 61-90 days: 25%
- 91-120 days: 50%
- Over 120 days: 100%

*(Adjust based on historical loss experience)*

#### 2.2.4 Fixed Assets (PAS 16)

**Capitalization Threshold:**
- Minimum amount: **PHP 20,000** per item
- Useful life: Greater than **1 year**

**Depreciation Methods:**
- **Straight-Line Method** (preferred for simplicity)
- Useful lives by asset class:
  - Buildings: 25-40 years
  - Office equipment: 5 years
  - Computers/IT equipment: 3 years
  - Furniture and fixtures: 5 years
  - Vehicles: 5 years
  - Leasehold improvements: Shorter of lease term or useful life

### 2.3 Internal Control Framework (SOX-Aligned)

#### 2.3.1 Segregation of Duties

**Key Principles:**
1. **Authorization:** Approval to execute transactions (by authorized personnel)
2. **Custody:** Physical or electronic access to assets
3. **Recording:** Entry of transactions into accounting system
4. **Reconciliation:** Verification and review of transactions

**No single person should have control over more than one of these functions.**

**Example - Cash Disbursement:**
- Authorization: Finance Director approves payment
- Custody: Treasury releases funds
- Recording: AP Clerk posts transaction
- Reconciliation: Bank reconciliation by separate staff

#### 2.3.2 Approval Authority Matrix

| Transaction Type | Amount Threshold | Approver 1 | Approver 2 | Approver 3 |
|------------------|------------------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Routine Journal Entries** | Any amount | Preparer | Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Accruals** | <PHP 50,000 | Preparer | Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Accruals** | >PHP 50,000 | Preparer + Dept Head | Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Prior Period Adjustments** | Any amount | Preparer | Supervisor | Finance Director (with memo) |
| **Bad Debt Write-Off** | Any amount | Project Accountant | Finance Supervisor | Finance Director + CFO |
| **Asset Capitalization** | <PHP 100,000 | Asset Accountant | - | Finance Director |
| **Asset Capitalization** | >PHP 100,000 | Asset Accountant | Finance Director | Executive Committee |
| **Reclassifications** | <PHP 50,000 | Preparer | Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Reclassifications** | >PHP 50,000 | Preparer (with memo) | Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Tax Payments** | Any amount | Tax Specialist | Finance Supervisor | Finance Director |
| **Period Close/Lock** | N/A | - | - | Finance Director (exclusive) |

#### 2.3.3 Access Controls

**ERP/Accounting System Access:**
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Users granted access based on job function
- **Least Privilege Principle:** Users have minimum access necessary
- **Posting Rights:** Limited to Finance Director or designated System Administrator
- **Read-Only Access:** For non-finance users (e.g., Project Managers, Department Heads)

**Access Review:**
- Quarterly review of user access rights
- Immediate revocation upon employee termination or role change

#### 2.3.4 Change Management

**Chart of Accounts Changes:**
- Requires Finance Director approval
- Document rationale and mapping to old accounts
- Update all templates and reports

**System Configuration Changes:**
- Requires Finance Director and IT approval
- Document change request with business justification
- Test in non-production environment first
- Maintain change log

## 3. Audit Trail and Evidence Requirements

### 3.1 Required Supporting Documentation

**For All Journal Entries:**
1. **Source Document:** Invoice, contract, agreement, or other originating document
2. **Calculation Worksheet:** For accruals, depreciation, tax computations
3. **Approval Evidence:** Email approval, signed form, or system approval log
4. **Narration:** Clear description of transaction purpose and account impact
5. **Reconciliation:** For balance sheet accounts

**Quality Standards:**
- All documents must be legible and complete
- Electronic copies acceptable (scanned PDFs)
- Naming convention: `[Date]_[Account]_[Description]_[Amount].pdf`

### 3.2 Documentation by Transaction Type

| Transaction Type | Required Documentation |
|------------------|------------------------|
| **Payroll** | Approved payroll register, government contribution computation, timesheets (if applicable) |
| **Rent/Lease** | Lease agreement, rental invoice or accrual calculation, payment confirmation |
| **Accruals** | Purchase order or contract, accrual calculation worksheet, department head approval email |
| **Depreciation** | Fixed asset register, depreciation schedule, asset acquisition documentation |
| **Client Billing** | Approved billing request form, job order or contract, POC calculation (if applicable) |
| **WIP Adjustment** | WIP schedule, Project Manager POC confirmation email, variance analysis |
| **Tax Returns** | BIR form, supporting schedules (sales/purchases register), payment voucher, bank confirmation |
| **Prior Period Adjustment** | Justification memo, original transaction reference, Finance Director approval |
| **Asset Capitalization** | Purchase invoice, asset requisition form, useful life determination memo, Executive Committee approval (if >PHP 100k) |
| **Bad Debt Write-Off** | AR aging, collection efforts documentation, customer communication, Finance Director + CFO approval |

### 3.3 Reconciliation Requirements

**Monthly Reconciliations (Mandatory):**
1. **Bank Reconciliations:** All bank accounts to bank statements
2. **AR Sub-Ledger to GL:** Accounts receivable control account
3. **AP Sub-Ledger to GL:** Accounts payable control account
4. **FA Sub-Ledger to GL:** Fixed assets and accumulated depreciation
5. **Inventory (if applicable):** Inventory control account
6. **WIP to GL:** Work-in-progress and unbilled receivables
7. **Intercompany Accounts:** With sister companies
8. **Tax Accounts:** VAT Input, VAT Output, Withholding Tax Payable

**Reconciliation Standards:**
- Completed by preparer within 5 business days of month-end
- Reviewed and signed off by supervisor
- Approved by Finance Director
- Variances >PHP 10,000 require investigation and resolution
- Reconciling items >60 days require action plan

## 4. Retention and Archival Policy

### 4.1 Document Retention Periods

| Document Category | Retention Period | Storage Medium | Disposal Method |
|-------------------|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **BIR Tax Returns** | **10 years** | Electronic + Physical | Shred physical; secure delete electronic |
| **VAT Invoices/Receipts** | **10 years** | Electronic + Physical | Shred physical; secure delete electronic |
| **Financial Statements** | **Permanent** | Electronic + Physical | Archive indefinitely |
| **General Ledger** | **Permanent** | Electronic | Archive indefinitely |
| **Journal Entries** | **7 years** | Electronic | Secure delete |
| **Supporting Documents** | **7 years** | Electronic | Secure delete |
| **Bank Statements** | **7 years** | Electronic + Physical | Shred physical; secure delete electronic |
| **Payroll Records** | **10 years** | Electronic | Secure delete |
| **Fixed Asset Register** | **Life of asset + 5 years** | Electronic | Secure delete |
| **Contracts/Agreements** | **Life of contract + 7 years** | Electronic + Physical | Shred physical; secure delete electronic |
| **Audit Reports** | **Permanent** | Electronic + Physical | Archive indefinitely |

### 4.2 Archival Process

**Month-End Close Documentation:**
1. Create monthly close folder: `Close_[YYYY-MM]`
2. Subfolders by category:
   - `01_Journal_Entries`
   - `02_Reconciliations`
   - `03_Tax_Returns`
   - `04_Schedules_and_Reports`
   - `05_Approvals`
   - `06_Correspondence`
3. Upload all documents within 3 business days of period close
4. Lock folder (read-only) after verification
5. Backup to secure offsite storage

**Naming Conventions:**
- Journal Entries: `JE_[YYYY-MM-DD]_[Account]_[Amount].pdf`
- Reconciliations: `Recon_[YYYY-MM]_[Account].xlsx`
- Tax Returns: `BIR_[Form]_[Period].pdf`

## 5. Controls and Audit Requirements

### 5.1 Key Controls

**Preventive Controls:**
1. **System-Enforced Approvals:** Transactions cannot post without required approvals
2. **Period Lock:** Prior periods automatically locked to prevent backdating
3. **Duplicate Invoice Check:** System flags potential duplicate vendor invoices
4. **Three-Way Match:** Purchase order, receipt, invoice match for AP processing
5. **Budget vs. Actual Alerts:** Flag expenses exceeding budget by >10%

**Detective Controls:**
1. **Monthly Bank Reconciliations:** Detect errors or fraud
2. **Variance Analysis:** Identify unusual trends or errors
3. **Aging Reports:** Identify overdue receivables or payables
4. **Reconciliations:** Detect discrepancies between sub-ledgers and GL
5. **Tax Compliance Review:** Verify all returns filed on time

**Corrective Controls:**
1. **Exception Escalation Process:** Defined in SOP and RACI
2. **Root Cause Analysis:** For material errors or control failures
3. **Process Improvement:** Quarterly review and update of procedures

### 5.2 Internal Audit Requirements

**Monthly Close Audit:**
- Internal Audit reviews sample of transactions quarterly
- Focus areas: High-risk accounts, new processes, prior findings

**Audit Sample Selection:**
- All transactions >PHP 500,000
- Random sample of 5% of transactions between PHP 50,000 - PHP 500,000
- Random sample of 1% of transactions <PHP 50,000

**Audit Testing:**
1. **Completeness:** All transactions recorded
2. **Accuracy:** Correct amounts and accounts
3. **Validity:** Supported by proper documentation
4. **Authorization:** Approved per policy
5. **Timeliness:** Recorded in correct period

### 5.3 External Audit Coordination

**Annual Audit:**
- Coordinate with external auditors for year-end audit
- Provide audit schedules per requested PBC (Provided by Client) list
- Typical PBC requests:
  - Trial balance
  - Lead schedules (by account)
  - Bank confirmations
  - AR/AP aging
  - Fixed asset register
  - Loan amortization schedules
  - Tax returns and payments
  - Minutes of Board meetings

**Quarterly Reviews (if applicable):**
- Limited review procedures for interim financial statements

## 6. Compliance Calendar

### 6.1 Monthly Compliance Activities

**By 5th Business Day:**
- ✅ Month-end close complete
- ✅ All journal entries posted and approved
- ✅ All reconciliations completed

**By 10th of Month:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 1601-C (Compensation withholding)
- ✅ File BIR Form 0619-E (Government remittances)
- ✅ File BIR Form 1601-E (EWT)
- ✅ Remit all withholding tax payments

**By 20th of Month:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 2550M (Monthly VAT)
- ✅ Remit VAT payment (if payable)

**By Month-End:**
- ✅ Issue financial statements to management
- ✅ Complete variance analysis
- ✅ Archive all supporting documentation

### 6.2 Quarterly Compliance Activities

**By 25th of Month Following Quarter:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 2550-Q (Quarterly VAT)
- ✅ Remit quarterly VAT payment

**By Last Day of Month Following Quarter:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 1702-Q (Quarterly Income Tax)
- ✅ Remit quarterly income tax payment
- ✅ File BIR Form 1701-Q (if applicable)

**Quarterly Close:**
- ✅ Conduct comprehensive analytical review
- ✅ Update forecasts and budgets
- ✅ Prepare Board reporting package

### 6.3 Annual Compliance Activities

**By January 31:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 2316 (Certificate of Compensation)
- ✅ File BIR Form 1604-CF (Annual Withholding on Compensation)
- ✅ File BIR Form 1604-E (Annual EWT)
- ✅ Issue 2316 to employees

**By April 15:**
- ✅ File BIR Form 1702 (Annual Income Tax Return)

## 7. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 7.1 Materiality Thresholds

**For Exception Escalation:**
- **Low:** <PHP 10,000 → Resolve at preparer/supervisor level
- **Medium:** PHP 10,000 - PHP 100,000 → Escalate to Finance Director
- **High:** >PHP 100,000 → Escalate to Finance Director + CFO

**For Financial Statement Impact:**
- **Trivial:** <0.5% of total assets or revenue → Note only
- **Material:** 0.5% - 5% of total assets or revenue → Require adjustment
- **Highly Material:** >5% of total assets or revenue → Immediate escalation to CFO/CEO

### 7.2 Escalation Matrix

| Issue | Timeline | First Escalation | Second Escalation | Final Escalation |
|-------|----------|------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Missing documentation | 24 hours | Finance Supervisor | Finance Director | CFO (if impacts close) |
| Reconciliation variance >PHP 10k | 48 hours | Finance Supervisor | Finance Director | - |
| Client billing dispute | 72 hours | Project Director | Finance Director | CFO + CEO |
| System/GL error | 4 hours | IT Support | Finance Director + IT Manager | CFO + CTO |
| Missed BIR deadline | Immediate | Finance Director | CFO | CEO + Legal |
| Fraud or irregularity | Immediate | Finance Director | CFO + Internal Audit | CEO + Board Audit Committee |

### 7.3 Incident Reporting

**For All Escalated Issues:**
1. Document in Incident Log
2. Prepare Incident Report with:
   - Description of issue
   - Root cause analysis
   - Financial impact
   - Corrective actions taken
   - Preventive measures for future
3. Submit to Finance Director within 48 hours of resolution
4. Follow up in monthly close review meeting

## 8. Governance and Oversight

### 8.1 Roles and Responsibilities

**Board of Directors / Audit Committee:**
- Oversee financial reporting integrity
- Review and approve audited financial statements
- Monitor compliance with laws and regulations
- Receive quarterly reports from CFO

**Chief Financial Officer (CFO):**
- Ultimate accountability for financial reporting
- Approve significant accounting policies and estimates
- Sign off on annual financial statements
- Escalation point for material issues

**Finance Director (GVKC):**
- Day-to-day oversight of month-end close
- Approve all journal entries and reconciliations
- Lock GL period
- Issue monthly financial statements
- Monitor compliance with this policy

**Finance Supervisors / Senior Accountants:**
- Execute month-end close procedures per SOP
- Review work of staff accountants
- First-line quality control
- Identify and escalate issues

**Internal Audit:**
- Independent review of controls and procedures
- Test compliance with policies
- Report findings to Audit Committee
- Recommend improvements

### 8.2 Policy Review and Updates

**Annual Review:**
- Conducted by Finance Director in Q1 of each year
- Incorporate regulatory changes (BIR, PFRS)
- Address audit findings and recommendations
- Update for process improvements

**Approval:**
- Finance Director prepares updated policy
- CFO reviews and approves
- Board/Audit Committee ratifies (for significant changes)

**Communication:**
- All finance staff trained on updates within 30 days
- Updated policy posted to document management system
- Acknowledgment of receipt required from all staff
